Dialer Types Explained
Salesloft uses power dialing (sequential, one call at a time). Here is the full landscape for 2026.
1. Power Dialing (Sequential)
Dials numbers one at a time in a queue. Best for AEs working warm or high-intent leads where quality matters over raw volume.
Best for: AEs, warm leads~60–80 dials/day
Platforms
Salesloft Apollo.io Revenue.io Close CRM Outreach Freshsales
2. Parallel Dialing (Predictive)
Dials 3–9 numbers simultaneously. AI detects live picks vs. voicemail. Delivers 3–5x more live conversations per hour.
Best for: SDRs, cold outbound3–5x more connects/hour
Platforms & Pricing
Nooks.ai — Best-in-class. $12,000–$20,000/yr (20 users). AI answer detection + coaching agents.
Outreach.io — Parallel + power combo. $18,000–$36,000+/yr. Enterprise-grade.
Orum — Call-acceleration specialist. Custom pricing. Auto-logs to CRM.
Outplay — Parallel + WhatsApp. $14,400–$16,800/yr. Unique for global teams.
3. Preview Dialing
Rep sees full prospect profile before the system dials. Prioritizes personalization over speed. Best for enterprise AEs on complex, high-value accounts.
Best for: Enterprise AEsQuality over volume
Talkdesk — Enterprise preview dialing. Custom pricing. Best for high-ACV deals needing deep prep.
Outreach — Preview mode available alongside SDR parallel dialing.
4. Manual / Click-to-Dial (No Dialer)
Rep manually clicks to dial. No automation. Lowest friction to set up, lowest call volume output.
Best for: Email-heavy teams~20–40 dials/day max
